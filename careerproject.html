Job description

A firefighter's main job is to fight fires although part of the job is helping injured people. They help sick and trapped people. There are many different jobs when someone is a member of a fire department. There are paramedics, engineers, regular firefighters. Paramedics are the ones who drive the ambulances and they treat injured people. Engineers are the ones who drive the fire engines. Normal firefighters ride on the engines and they are the ones who put out the fires. Firefighters do more than just fight fires but they do that also.

Salary

  • Median pay (annually): $46,870

  • Median pay (hourly): $22,53

  • Projected 10 year growth: 5%

Relation to ECS

Computer science is not directly related to firefighting but they tie together. Computer scientists are always working on new technology for firefighters to make their jobs easier and safer. For example, firefighters go off of GPS when they go into a fire but computer scientists have found a way to fix that with a new GPS tracking system.

Required education

  • High school diploma
  • Fire training academy
  • On-the-job training
  • EMT certification is common
